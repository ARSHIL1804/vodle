About maxparc

maxparc helps groups find their maximal partial consensus among any set of options.

For each option, it shows you the current support as a grey bar on the right, and allows you to adjust your ratings in realtime. To help you converge to a full or partial consensus as broad as possible, options are listed by descending support.

When the poll closes, the winning option is determined by a fair algorithm that gives every voter the same influence, not just those voters who happen to belong to the majority.

If one option is supported by all, this full consensus option will be the sure winner. Otherwise maxparc most likely declares the option with the broadest support the winner. But to achieve fairness, it must sometimes also use a certain amount of chance, similar to a greencard lottery.

The exact procedure that maxparc uses for determining the winning option is this: After the poll has closed, a voter is picked by lot. Then the topmost option for which that voter's rating knob overlaps with the grey support bar is declared the winner.

This way, the most supported option will have the vastly largest probability of winning, and all other options' winning probability is at most as large as their support.
