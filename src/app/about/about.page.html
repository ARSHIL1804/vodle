What is vodle?

vodle helps a group make fair and good decisions. It finds the maximal partial consensus among any set of options. Truly democratic, it gives everyone the same effective influence.

For each option, it shows you the current approval as a light bar on the right, and allows you to adjust your ratings in realtime using sliders. When your slider touches the approval bar, it turns green to indicate that you approve that option as well. This way, the group tends to converge to a full or partial consensus as broad as possible.

When the poll closes, the winning option is determined by a fair but simple algorithm that gives every voter the same influence, not just those voters who happen to be in a majority.

  In choice polls (where your group is selecting a single option), the decision is made as follows. If one option is approved by all, this full consensus option will be the sure winner. Otherwise vodle most likely declares the option with the broadest approval the winner. But to achieve fairness, it must sometimes also use a certain amount of chance, similar to a greencard lottery.

The exact procedure that vodle uses for determining the winning option is this: After the poll has closed, options are sorted by descending approval and one voter is picked by lot. The top-most option for which that voter's slider is green is declared the winner.

This way, the most-approved option will have the vastly largest probability of winning. Other options' winning probabilities are at most as large as their approval scores (in percent of all voters who do not abstain).

  In allocation polls (where your group is dividing some budget or resource into shares for several targets or recipients), the procedure is similar: If one target is approved by all, you have full consensus, so this target will get the full budget. Otherwise vodle allocates the largest share of the budget to the target with the broadest approval, and smaller shares to the other targets.

Exact procedure: Targets are sorted by descending approval. Then each target's share is proportional to the number of voters who approve this target but no higher-up target.

Who's behind it?

vodle's algorithm was developed and optimizied for fairness by the mathematician Jobst Heitzig, inspired by the "threshold model" of famous sociologist Mark Granovetter, in collaboration with Forest W. Simmons. This app is open-source, code hosted on github. Main developer: Jobst Heitzig. Contributors: Marius Amrhein.
